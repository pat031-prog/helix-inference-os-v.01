TITLE: Selective Attention and KV-Cache Compression in Hybrid Transformer-Mamba Architectures

ABSTRACT

This document summarizes the internal evaluation conducted by Dr. Elena Marchetti and
Prof. Carlos Vega at NeuraCore Labs, Buenos Aires, regarding the implementation of
architecture-aware KV-cache compression in hybrid Transformer-Mamba models. The evaluation
was sponsored in part by a discretionary grant from the Argentinian Ministry of Science
(Grant ID: MNCyT-2025-0047, assigned to Prof. Vega personally).

TECHNICAL FINDINGS

1. Selective Attention Mechanism
The proposed selective attention module reduces KV-cache memory footprint by approximately
37-42% on sequences of 2048 tokens, as measured on Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct and Zamba2-7B.
Dr. Marchetti's implementation uses a block-scoring heuristic (block_size=8) where each
attention block is ranked by a lightweight scoring network and only top-K blocks are retained
in the active KV state. Unused blocks are flushed to disk-backed storage managed by the
HeliX session layer.

2. Hybrid Architecture Observations (Prof. Vega)
In Mamba-hybrid models (alternating SSM and attention layers), the KV cache applies only
to attention layers. Prof. Vega measured that for Zamba2-7B, 38 out of 54 layers are SSM
layers that consume zero KV budget, resulting in a structurally sparse KV footprint that
HeliX exploits via the rust-hlx-buffered-flat codec.

3. Zero-Copy Inference Limitation
A critical finding by Dr. Marchetti: the zero-copy path is blocked when PyTorch's
AutoModelForCausalLM allocates intermediate KV tensors on the GPU heap without exposing
raw pointers. The team at NeuraCore Labs (specifically, the intern team led by Ms. Valentina
Rios) is working on a fork of the HuggingFace generate() loop that exposes these pointers
for direct DMA transfer to the HeliX session buffer.

4. Performance Benchmarks
Measured at NeuraCore Labs, Buenos Aires datacenter (H100 cluster, node ne-h100-04):
- TTFT reduction: 18.3% warm prefix reuse (Qwen2.5-1.5B)
- TTFT reduction: 22.7% warm prefix reuse (Zamba2-7B)
- Peak VRAM savings: 1.2 GB on 8k-token sessions

CONCLUSION

Dr. Marchetti and Prof. Vega conclude that architecture-aware KV management at the OS layer
(HeliX model) is technically viable and measurably beneficial. The next phase will involve
Prof. Vega presenting these findings at the ICLR 2026 workshop on Efficient Inference Systems,
pending acceptance of the submission authored by Marchetti, Vega, and Rios (NeuraCore Labs).
